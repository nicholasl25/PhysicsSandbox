3D gravity simulation — demos

Screen captures from the web sim: orbits, integrator comparison, and visuals.

The sim supports several time-stepping schemes. Plain Euler adds energy quickly and blows up orbits; semi-implicit Euler and Verlet stay much closer to the true trajectory for the same step size. Prefer semi-implicit Euler or Verlet for long or fast runs. RK4 (Runge–Kutta 4th order) tracks orbits accurately in many cases, but it is not symplectic, so total energy tends to drift over very long runs; it also does more work per step, so it may handle fewer bodies smoothly than semi-implicit Euler or Verlet at the same frame rate.
